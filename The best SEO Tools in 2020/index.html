The best SEO Tools in 2020 That Get The Job Done (Backed up by real users)

05 October 2020 2344

SEO growth can be a hard and tedious job. You have to thoroughly analyze the market and your competitors if you want to rank higher on Google Search Results.

I compiled this list based on experienced redditors who built up real businesses and know what they're talking about. I've also included some tips and tricks to make it easier to get started in case it's your first time hearing about these tools.

So, let's get started!

1. Moz

You will hear A LOT about this SEO tool. It has a bunch of features that come in handy, but there's especially one that I'm fond of: MozBar.
It's a free Chrome extension that lets you check the Domain Authority, Page Authority, Backlinks, Tags, Markup and much more! All you have to do is click the extension once you open a competitor's website and start analyzing what makes them successful.

Here's how to get started:

Step 1: Click the link above and install the Chrome Extension.

Step 2: Click the extension once you're at your competitor's website, it will show a narrow bar below your bookmarks tab.

Step 3: Click on either of the icons below and start exploring! Try to experiment a bit, it's the only way to get used and learn more about this SEO Tool!

Note: Moz and their app suite have paid services like Rank Tracking, Backlink Analysis, Keyword Research and Site Audits. If you're just learning , first you need to make sure you're comfortable using this and other SEO Tools. After you spend some time learning the basics, by any means try the pro (paid) version. They have a 30-day trial similar to Amazon that you can see for youself and renew payment if you think they're worth it. Support the developers for their hard work!
